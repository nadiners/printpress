Welcome

In an effort to bridge the gap between designers and digital printers the Print/Press project will commission 12 independent designers to produce a 2019 calendar. Developments in digital printing technology have introduced workflows taking advantage of Variable Data to customise design elements (text, colour, image and vector) within a print run. This technique brings with it the so-called personalisation revolution allowing multiple objects within a single print run to be branded using customer data.

While the take up of variable data and personalisation by big brands is widespread, our research shows that there is little evidence of these techniques being used by independent designers and artists. In the era of big data, it is the contention of this project that work needs to be done to critically reflect on the implications of these techniques for the consumer.

To this end we will use the format of a typical example of personalisation merchandise – a 2019 calendar – to invite 12 independent designers (one designer for each calendar month) to work with variable data techniques and to critically reflect on the so-called personalisation revolution.
